Section I

Bitcoin Miners

Terahash (TH)

A miner's performance is measured in hashes (calculations) per second. A 200 TH miner performs 200 trillion hashes per second.

Watts per Terahash (w/TH)

A miner's efficiency is rated by watts per terahash. Less is more — a lower w/TH rating means more efficient, more profitable mining.

Difficulty

Every week, the Bitcoin algorithm automatically adjusts how difficult it is to solve a block, targeting an average of 10 minutes per block.

  • When more hashpower joins the network, blocks are solved faster — difficulty adjusts up
  • When miners go offline, difficulty adjusts down
  • As difficulty rises, efficiency becomes increasingly critical to profitability

Price per TH

Miners are marketed by dollars per TH, but this does not reflect efficiency and can be misleading.

Key Insight A miner rated at 30 w/TH might cost pennies per TH, while a miner rated at 10 w/TH could cost $20–$30 per TH. Always evaluate efficiency alongside price.

Where to Buy

Manufacturers like Bitmain, MicroBT, and Bitdeer sell directly at MSRP, but online distributors or hosting facilities often have better deals.

  • cryptominerbros.com
  • asicmarketplace.com
  • apextomining.com

Hosting facilities are often more convenient for buyers who don't have access to cheap electricity at home.

Section II

Hosting Companies

Benefits

Skip shipping costs, tariffs, and sometimes sales tax — while avoiding the heat, noise, and maintenance of running an ASIC miner yourself.

Electric Rate

Most hosting facilities charge 8 cents per kilowatt or less. 7–7.5 cents/kWh is common, but usually depends on how many units are ordered.

Taxes & Fees

When shopping at hosting facilities, check all fees by adding miners to cart and going through checkout to reveal any deposits, setup fees, taxes, and credit card surcharges before committing.

Location

Avoid Texas — heat and grid instability make it a poor choice for hosting.

The USA and Canada have plenty of cool-weather regions for hosting, with the industry constantly expanding into new areas.

Used Market

The longer a unit exists, the more important it becomes to sell it during more profitable periods. Good hosting facilities will also have a thriving secondary market for used miners.

Mining Pool

Every miner connects to the Bitcoin network through a mining pool. A hosting facility will ask for your mining pool account name and network address. The top 4–5 pools make this process simple and easy to maintain.
